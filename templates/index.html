Physics

Albert Einstein

Theoretical Physicist

“I seek simplicity in the fabric of reality.”

German-born physicist who revolutionized our understanding of space, time, and energy. His work reshaped modern physics and continues to influence science and technology.

  • Special and General Relativity
  • Photoelectric Effect (Nobel Prize, 1921)
  • Mass–Energy Equivalence (E = mc^2)
Mathematics • Physics

Isaac Newton

Mathematician and Natural Philosopher

“If I have seen further, it is by standing on the shoulders of giants.”

English polymath whose laws of motion and universal gravitation laid the foundations of classical mechanics, along with pivotal contributions to calculus and optics.

  • Laws of Motion and Universal Gravitation
  • Infinitesimal Calculus (co-creator)
  • Corpuscular Theory of Light and Prism Experiments
Mathematics • Astronomy

Aryabhatta

Mathematician and Astronomer

“Numbers reveal the rhythm of the cosmos.”

Ancient Indian mathematician-astronomer whose treatise Aryabhatiya presented foundational results in arithmetic, algebra, and celestial models, centuries ahead of his time.

  • Approximation of π and place-value system usage
  • Trigonometric functions: sine (jya) and cosine (kojya)
  • Heliocentric insights and Earth's rotation concepts
